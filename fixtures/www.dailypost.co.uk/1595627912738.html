More than 40 Covid patients in Wrexham hospital as town records most new cases

The figure compares to just five at Glan Clwyd and none at Ysbyty Gwynedd

  • Share
  •  
  •  Comments
By
Liam Randall
  • 10:29, 24 JUL 2020
News

More than 40 people are currently in hospital in Wrexham battling coronavirus.

The news comes after it emerged that 34 people have tested positive for COVID-19 in the town in the last seven days, including nine in the last 24 hours - the highest rate in any part of Wales.

Meanwhile, the county borough has been placed second on a list of the ten local authority areas with the highest estimates of symptomatic COVID in the past week.

While there are more than 40 in hospital with coronavirus in the town, there are only five at Ysbyty Glan Clwyd and none at Ysbyty Gwynedd.

Chris Stockport, executive director for primary and community care, said: "In terms of our inpatient activity, in Ysbyty Gwynedd in Bangor the levels of inpatient numbers are low and as of today we have no patients with coronavirus in Ysbyty Gwynedd.

"In Glan Clwyd, the numbers have been quite high but in recent weeks have fallen quite quickly.

"We are currently experiencing a little bit of heightened activity in the Wrexham area. That's both in the community and in hospital.

"We're actively managing that, but it means the inpatient numbers in Wrexham have not yet fallen to the same sort of numbers that we're seeing in Glan Clwyd."

Wrexham recently experienced a localised outbreak of the coronavirus associated with a ready meals manufacturing plant.

The total number of positive cases linked to the Rowan Foods factory on the town's industrial estate currently stands at 313.

Public Health Wales said the outbreak remains active, but with only a small increase of four cases in the last week.

Health board chair Mark Polin suggested there was a possibility that some people may have contracted the virus in hospital and has asked for a review to be carried out to prevent it from spreading among staff and patients.

Betsi Cadwaladr has now confirmed it is asking everyone to wear either a mask or face covering within hospitals.

Simon Dean, interim chief executive said: "We take our infection prevention and control responsibilities very seriously and are doing all we can to reduce the risk of COVID-19 infection in our hospitals amongst both our staff and patients.

"We have taken a range of actions to prevent the spread of the virus including testing all patients who are due to come in for planned surgery before they are admitted to hospital.

"As of this week, all admissions - including emergencies - across the health board will be tested."

Mr Dean said a number of steps had been taken to stop the spread, including training staff on infection prevention, undertaking extra cleaning and increasing the number of hand sanitiser stations.

Visiting restrictions have also been imposed, as staffing levels have been boosted on elderly wards.

He also urged people to observe good hand hygiene, maintain social distancing and not to visit healthcare premises if displaying any symptoms of COVID-19.

Mr Dean said: "We are the largest health board in Wales, covering 22 per cent of the Welsh population and have had more cases of COVID-19 than other health boards.

"COVID-19 is highly infectious and it can be challenging to prevent the spread of the virus.

"We are not complacent and we are building on what we have learned to date.

"We are actively encouraging patients, the public and staff to wear face coverings or masks in public areas of all health board sites and are actively exploring repeat testing of patients seven days after a negative test to improve surveillance of the virus on our sites."

Public Health Wales officials said an outbreak control team recently came together to review the Rowan Foods outbreak and was constantly monitoring infection data.

They added that cases in Wrexham were being investigated through the Test Trace Protect process.

Incident director Dr Robin Howe said: "The outbreak associated with the workforce at the Rowan Foods plant in Wrexham remains active, although the number of reported cases has slowed down considerably.

"As of 22 July, the total number of positive cases associated with this outbreak is 313, which is a small increase of four since Wednesday 15 July. There is no evidence that these infections were acquired either onsite or that the infection is factory-based.

"We will continue to analyse the data to ensure we are taking appropriate and timely decisions to bring the outbreak to a close."

Commenting on the data within the COVID Symptom Study Watch List, Tim Spector, Professor of Genetic Epidemiology at King's College London, said: "We are now starting to see the figures in the north creep up week on week.

"The numbers are still very small so we can't say with 100 per cent certainty that COVID is increasing significantly but it does make us focus on those areas."
